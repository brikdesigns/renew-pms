Renew uses two distinct role layers. Keep them separate in your mental model.

## Layer 1: System role — permission tier

Stored on `profiles.system_role`. This is the **modifier** — it determines your permission tier, not your identity. An Owner has admin access. A Clinical Manager can have admin access. A Dental Assistant has staff access. The modifier is independent of what your job is.

| System role | Who gets it | Access |
|-------------|-------------|--------|
| `brik_admin` | Brik staff | Full access across all practices |
| `admin` | Owners, Office Managers, and anyone the practice designates as admin | Practice-wide access — settings, all departments, all work items |
| `manager` | Clinical Managers, Lead BAs, and department leads | Department-level elevated access — triage, approve, team metrics. No settings. |
| `staff` | Standard team members | Scoped to their own work and their department |

System role is a `text` column with a `CHECK` constraint — app logic depends on these values. Not user-renameable.

**Key insight:** "Admin" is not a job function. It's a capability modifier applied to any role. An Owner is an admin. A Manager can be an admin. The `system_role` field carries this — practice roles never should.

Each practice role has a `default_system_role` column that suggests the appropriate permission tier when inviting a new user. The admin can always override this at invite time.

## Layer 2: Practice role — job function

Stored via `practice_members.practice_role_id` → `practice_role_types`. This is **what you are** — your job function at the practice.

Current seeded roles (from Notion content mapping spec):

| Role | Department | Default tier | Description |
|------|-----------|:---:|-------------|
| (O) Owner | Leadership | `admin` | Business owner. Ultimate decision-maker. |
| (D) Doctor | Clinical | `admin` | Licensed dentist. Diagnosis, treatment planning, care delivery. |
| Office Manager | Management | `admin` | Day-to-day admin and operations. Liaison between leadership and staff. |
| Lead Business Administrator | Business Administration | `manager` | Senior front-office. Manages the BA team. |
| Business Administrator | Business Administration | `staff` | Scheduling, billing, administrative support. |
| Insurance Coordinator | Business Administration | `staff` | Claims, verifications, insurance billing. Also spans Finance. |
| Clinical Manager | Clinical | `manager` | Supervises clinical ops and staff. Quality and compliance. Also spans Management. |
| (H) Dental Hygienist | Clinical | `staff` | Preventive oral health care and patient education. |
| (A) Dental Assistant | Clinical | `staff` | Supports doctors and hygienists in procedures. |
| Third Party | IT | `staff` | External vendors, contractors, consultants. Also spans Finance, Marketing, Facilities. |
| Everyone | (G) All Departments | `staff` | Applies to all team members. Used for practice-wide task/template assignment. |

Practice roles live in a **reference table** — practice admins can rename, add, or deactivate them. Never hardcode role name values in app logic.

**What doesn't belong here:** Generic labels like "Admin", "Staff", or "Manager" without a department context. These describe permission levels or vague organizational tiers, not job functions. If a practice needs a "Manager" role, it should be scoped — "Clinical Manager", "Front Office Manager", etc.

### Role → department → color

Each role has a `department_id` FK pointing to `departments`. The department's `color` column (e.g. `"blue"`, `"gold"`) drives UI color throughout the app:

- **Role card avatars** use the department's light color as the background
- **User card avatars** inherit color through: `practice_members.practice_role_id` → `practice_role_types.department_id` → `departments.color`
- **Department tags** on user cards use the same light + text token pair

This is a single FK chain — changing a department's color automatically updates every role and user that belongs to it. See [Design Tokens](/docs/architecture/design-tokens) for the full color derivation chain and token reference.

## Department scoping

Departments determine **what's relevant to you**. System role determines **what you're allowed to see**.

Current seeded departments:

| Department | Color | Scope |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| (G) All Departments | taupe | Global — members bypass department filtering |
| (L) Leadership | gold | Global — practice-wide visibility |
| (M) Management | purple | Global — practice-wide visibility |
| (BA) Business Administration | green | Department-scoped |
| (C) Clinical | blue | Department-scoped |
| HR (Human Resources) | brown | Department-scoped |
| IT (Information Technology) | taupe | Department-scoped |
| Marketing | red | Department-scoped |
| Finance | gold | Department-scoped |
| Facilities | taupe | Department-scoped |

### Global departments

Some departments are inherently cross-departmental: Leadership, Management, and the "(G) All Departments" catch-all. Members of these departments are not filtered to a single department — they see work items across the practice.

**Rule:** When a user's practice role points to a global department, department-based content filtering is bypassed. Their visibility is practice-wide (subject to their system role tier).

Global visibility applies to: **(G) All Departments**, **(L) Leadership**, **(M) Management**

This will be formalized with an `is_global` flag on the `departments` table. Until then, the app checks department name conventions (prefix-based).

### Scoping matrix

| System role | Global department | Department-scoped |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| `admin` | Full practice access | Full practice access (admin trumps dept) |
| `manager` | Cross-department team oversight, no settings | Own department's team oversight, triage, approve |
| `staff` | Cross-department work items, no settings | Own department's work items only |

A Clinical Manager with `manager` system role sees Clinical department work items + team metrics, can triage requests and reassign tasks within Clinical. Promote to `admin` to grant settings access and practice-wide visibility.

## Feature permissions

Every feature is scoped by **system role** (permission tier) and **department/assignment** (relevance). The general rule: staff see only what's assigned to them or their department; admins see everything.

### Scoping dimensions

| Dimension | Source | Purpose |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| System role | `profiles.system_role` | Broad permission tier (admin vs staff) |
| Department | `practice_members` → `practice_role_types.department_id` | Content filtering — what's relevant |
| Global dept | Department `is_global` flag (Leadership, Management, All) | Bypasses department filtering |
| Assignment | `tasks.assignee_id`, `requests.assignee_id` | Direct ownership of work items |
| User | `profiles.id` | Individual-level filtering (notifications, dashboard) |

### Notifications

Scoped by role, department, and user. A staff member should never see notifications intended for admins.

| System role | Sees |
|-------------|------|
| `brik_admin` | All notifications across all practices |
| `admin` | All notifications within their practice |
| `manager` | Department-level notifications + direct (assigned/submitted) notifications |
| `staff` | Only direct notifications: assigned tasks, requests they submitted or are assigned to |

**Rules:**
- Admin-level notifications (new user signups, system alerts, settings changes) are never visible to `staff`
- Department-scoped notifications (e.g. "new task in Clinical") are visible to members of that department + members of global departments
- Direct notifications (task assigned, request status change) are visible to the assigned user and the submitter

### Dashboard

User-specific view based on role and department. Staff see their own work; admins see the full picture.

| System role | Dashboard content |
|-------------|-------------------|
| `brik_admin` | Cross-practice overview. All tasks, requests, and metrics. |
| `admin` | Practice-wide view. All tasks, requests, events, and team metrics. |
| `manager` | Department view. Tasks and requests for their department + their own. Team metrics for their department. |
| `staff` (dept-scoped) | Personal view. Tasks assigned to them, requests they submitted/assigned, events for their department. |
| `staff` (global dept) | Personal view + cross-department work items. Still no settings or admin features. |

**Rules:**
- Staff dashboard shows: my tasks, my requests, my department's events (or cross-dept if global)
- Admin dashboard shows: all tasks (filterable by assignee/department), all requests (full pipeline), all events, team-level metrics
- Counts and summaries reflect the scoped data — staff counts exclude items outside their scope

### Workday features (Tasks, Requests, Events)

Work items are scoped by assignment and department. Staff operate within their lane; admins have full visibility.

#### Tasks

| System role | Sees | Can do |
|-------------|------|--------|
| `brik_admin` | All tasks across all practices | Full CRUD, reassign anyone |
| `admin` | All tasks within their practice | Full CRUD, reassign anyone in practice |
| `manager` | Tasks in their department + tasks assigned to them | Update status, reassign within their department |
| `staff` (dept-scoped) | Tasks assigned to them or their department | Update status on assigned tasks, cannot reassign |
| `staff` (global dept) | Tasks across all departments | Update status on assigned tasks, cannot reassign |

#### Requests

Two distinct UI experiences based on system role (Option C):

**Admin / Manager view — Kanban board:**

| System role | Sees | Can do |
|-------------|------|--------|
| `brik_admin` | All requests across all practices | Full CRUD, assign, triage, status pipeline |
| `admin` | All requests within their practice | Full CRUD, assign, triage, status pipeline |
| `manager` | Requests in their department | Triage, assign within department, change status |

Admins and managers see the request board with status columns. This is an operational triage tool — assign requests, move them through the pipeline, manage the queue. Managers see a department-filtered view.

**Staff view — "My Requests" list:**

| System role | Sees | Can do |
|-------------|------|--------|
| `staff` | Requests they submitted + requests assigned to them | Submit new requests, add comments to their own, view status updates |

Staff see a flat list with status badges — not a board. This is a submit-and-track interface. They can see where their request is in the pipeline but cannot triage, reassign, or see other people's requests.

**Why two views:** The Kanban board is an operational tool for people managing the request queue. For a dental assistant who submits a broken equipment request once a month, a full board with mostly-empty columns is confusing. A simple "My Requests" list with status is the right UX. Different tools for different jobs.

#### Events

| System role | Sees |
|-------------|------|
| `brik_admin` | All events across all practices |
| `admin` | All events within their practice |
| `manager` | Events for their department + events they are directly invited to |
| `staff` (dept-scoped) | Events for their department + events they are directly invited to |
| `staff` (global dept) | Events across all departments + events they are directly invited to |

### Analytics

Visibility is tiered. Staff see personal and team-level metrics only; admins get the full analytics suite.

| System role | Sees |
|-------------|------|
| `brik_admin` | Full analytics across all practices |
| `admin` | Practice-wide analytics: all departments, all team members, all metrics |
| `manager` | Department analytics: team member metrics within their department, department-level summaries |
| `staff` | Personal metrics only: their task completion, request activity, and event participation. Department-level summaries for their own department (or cross-dept if global). |

**Rules:**
- Staff cannot see individual metrics for other team members
- Staff can see aggregate department metrics for departments they belong to (e.g. "Clinical department completed 42 tasks this week")
- Revenue, billing, and operational cost metrics are admin-only
- Admins can drill down to individual team member performance

### Settings

Admin-only with one exception. Staff see only their account settings.

| Setting page | `brik_admin` | `admin` | `manager` | `staff` |
|-------------|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| Account | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Organization | Yes | Yes | No | No |
| Departments | Yes | Yes | No | No |
| Roles | Yes | Yes | No | No |
| Users | Yes | Yes | No | No |
| Contacts | Yes | Yes | No | No |
| Inventory | Yes | Yes | No | No |
| Templates | Yes | Yes | No | No |

**Rules:**
- Non-admin users (`manager`, `staff`) navigating to any admin setting page are redirected to `/settings/account`
- The settings sub-nav hides admin-only items for `staff` users (already implemented via `adminOnly` flag on nav items)
- Within admin settings, `admin` has full control over their practice; `brik_admin` can access any practice

## Implementation notes

These rules are enforced at two levels:

1. **Server-side (RLS + API routes):** Supabase RLS policies and API route guards ensure data never leaks regardless of client behavior. This is the security boundary.
2. **Client-side (UI filtering):** Components conditionally render based on the user's system role and department membership. This is the UX layer — it shapes what staff see but is not the security enforcement.

**Key principle:** Never rely on client-side filtering alone for access control. Every data query must be scoped by RLS or API-level checks. The client UI reflects these boundaries but does not enforce them.

| Feature | Status | Notes |

| `isAdmin()` helper | Done | `src/lib/auth.ts`. Use instead of inline role checks. |

### Recommended: `get_user_context()` SQL function

Create a single `SECURITY DEFINER` function that returns `{ system_role, practice_id, department_id, is_global_dept, user_id }`. Use it in every RLS policy instead of repeating subqueries. This gives one place to extend when capability flags arrive in V2.

## V2 Roadmap — Granular feature permissions

The current three-tier model (`admin` / `manager` / `staff`) covers most dental practice needs. As the product matures, practices may need finer control: a manager who can approve requests but not access billing, an admin who can manage users but not delete templates.

### The gap

The three tiers handle 90% of cases, but the remaining 10% will need per-feature granularity. A Clinical Manager (`manager`) can triage within their department, but what if they also need read-only access to another department's analytics? That's where feature permissions come in.

### Planned model

A `feature_permissions` table that grants granular CRUD + visibility per feature, assignable to roles, departments, or individual users:

```
feature_permissions
  id              uuid
  practice_id     uuid
  target_type     text ('role' | 'department' | 'user')
  target_id       uuid (FK to role/dept/user)
  feature         text ('requests' | 'tasks' | 'analytics' | 'settings.users' | ...)
  can_view        boolean default false
  can_create      boolean default false
  can_edit        boolean default false
  can_delete      boolean default false
```

### Resolution order

When checking permissions for a user on a feature:

1. **User override** — most specific, wins if present
2. **Department permission** — applies to all members of a department
3. **Role permission** — applies to all members holding a practice role
4. **System role default** — `admin` gets full access, `manager` gets department access, `staff` gets scoped access

Each level can grant or restrict. More specific levels override less specific ones.

### When to build this

Not until: (a) the first client requests it, or (b) we have a concrete use case where the three tiers are genuinely blocking a workflow. The three-tier model covers 90% of dental practice needs. Build the 10% when the 10% asks for it.

### Preparing now

Even though V2 permissions are deferred, the V1 codebase should be ready:

- **Centralize permission checks** — use helper functions (`isAdmin()`, `canAccessFeature()`) not inline `system_role === 'admin'` checks. When V2 arrives, you update the helper, not every call site. The `isAdmin()` helper is already implemented in `src/lib/auth.ts`.
- **Build the `get_user_context()` SQL function** — one place to extend with capability flags later.
- **Keep system_role as the coarse gate** — V2 feature_permissions refine it, never replace it. RLS policies should always check system_role first, then layer on feature permissions.
